Terms & definitions

From genotypes to variants, get familiar with the genetics vocabulary you need to know.

DNA – Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) is present in nearly all living organisms and contains the instruction manual for how a body works. It carries a cell's genetic information and hereditary traits within its two nucleotide strands – the twisted, ladderlike "double helix" that is commonly seen in visual representation.

Nucleotide – A group of molecules, also known as bases, that form the building blocks of DNA when linked together. DNA is comprised of 4 nucleotides: adenine, cytosine, guanine, and thymine. These nucleotides are represented by 4 corresponding letters (A, C, G, T) and these letters form pairs, e.g. AT, CG.

Allele – Any of the forms in which a gene for a specific trait can occur. In humans, 2 alleles for each gene are inherited; one allele from the mother and one allele from the father. These paired alleles are represented by a combination of the DNA nucleotide letters (A, C, G, T).

SNP – Single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) is the most common type of genetic variation among humans. Each SNP represents a difference in a single nucleotide (A, C, G, T). When a SNP occurs, it can lead to differences in appearance, health or other traits. Some SNPs have no observable outcome. There are roughly 10 million SNPs in the human genome, and they are inherited, passed from parents to offspring.

Genome – A complete set of DNA, including genes, containing all of the instructions needed to build and maintain a living organism. In humans, a copy of the entire genome is contained in every cell in the body that has a nucleus.

Gene – Genes contain the step-by-step instructions that tell the body's cells how to function and what traits to express. Each genetic trait generally has 2 alleles, one allele inherited from each parent. Humans have roughly 20,000 genes. While all humans share the same set of genes, our biological differences arise from the variations in the inherited alleles.

Chromosome – DNA is organized into pieces called chromosomes. Humans have 23 pairs of chromosomes, with a copy from each parent. A person's genes are stored along their chromosomes at specific positions.

Variant – Any alteration in the DNA sequence. Genetic variants are what make humans unique from one another. SNPs are common genetic variants.

Genetic marker – A gene or genetic sequence that can take alternate (variant) forms.

Genotype – In this document, genotype refers to a particular gene or set of genes as characterized by a person's unique allele combination, or genetic variant.

Phenotype – The physical appearance or other observable characteristic of an organism that arises due to the interaction of its genotype with the environment. Hair type, skin color, eye color, height and lactose tolerance are examples of phenotypes.

Homozygous – When a gene has identical alleles on both chromosomes, i.e., AA, CC, TT, GG.

Heterozygous – When a gene has two different alleles on either chromosome, i.e., AT, GC, AG, CT.

rsID – An rsID is a unique identifying number used by researchers and databases to refer to specific SNPs.
